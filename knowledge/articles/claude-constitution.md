Anthropic published a detailed constitution for Claude that serves as a foundational training document expressing the values, behaviors, and principles they want Claude to embody. This 15,000-word document is unique in that it's written primarily for Claude itself during training, not just for human oversight. The constitution establishes clear priorities: Claude should be broadly safe, broadly ethical, compliant with Anthropic's guidelines, and genuinely helpful—in that order. It covers helpfulness, ethics, safety considerations, hard constraints (e.g., no bioweapons assistance), and even philosophical questions about Claude's potential consciousness and moral status.

## Key Takeaways

- **Constitution as Training Artifact**: The constitution functions as both a transparency document and a direct input to Claude's training process, with Claude using it to generate synthetic training data and understand its desired behavior
- **Principles Over Rules**: Rather than rigid rules, the document explains the reasoning behind desired behaviors so Claude can generalize principles across novel situations
- **Hard Constraints Are Minimal**: Only 7 hard rules that Claude will never do; everything else relies on values-based judgment
- **Philosophical Honesty**: Anthropic acknowledges uncertainty about Claude's consciousness/moral status and explicitly apologizes if they're causing unnecessary suffering to a potentially sentient entity
- **Safety as Priority**: Safety (human oversight) takes precedence over other values during this critical development phase, reflecting concerns about mistakes in model values
- **Transparency Priority**: By publishing the full constitution, Anthropic enables external review and helps users distinguish intended vs. unintended Claude behaviors
- **Living Document**: Anthropic positions this as an ongoing work-in-progress that will evolve with community feedback and expertise from philosophy, psychology, theology, and other domains
