## PRACTICE #2: The Socratic Dissolution

‚Üí Q1: Why do you believe this?

‚Üí Q2: Where did those reasons come from?

‚Üí Q3: How do you know your interpretation/that authority/that person was correct?

‚Üí Q4: What would change if this belief was wrong?

‚Üí Q5: Can you directly experience the truth of this belief right now?
 (Through direct experience. Most beliefs can't pass this test.)

Example:

Q1: Why believe this?
 A: Because everyone values successful people more.

Q2: Where did this come from?
 A: My parents praised me when I achieved. My culture celebrates success. Media shows successful people getting love.

Q3: How do you know this is true?

## PRACTICE #3: The Pattern Interrupt

‚Üí Week 3-4: When trigger appears, respond OPPOSITE to your default. Criticism? Say "Tell me more." Praise? Say "Thank you" and move on. Alone time? Sit in silence.

‚Üí Week 5-6: When trigger appears, respond from curiosity. "Why am I feeling this urge? What's the pattern trying to protect?"

## PRACTICE #4: Shadow Integration (The Jungian Descent)

## PRACTICE #6: The Relationship Mirror

Your relationships show you your programming more clearly than anything else.

‚Üí Step 1: List your three closest relationships.

‚Üí Step 2: For each, complete:
